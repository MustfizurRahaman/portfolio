About Me

I'm neck-deep in Deep Learning, Computer Vision, and Geospatial AI, developing algorithms that make sense of hyperspectral imagery. This data contains hundreds of spectral bands instead of the usual three. Yes, it's as chaotic as it sounds. As a PhD researcher in Geo-Analytics, I work on self-supervised learning, physics-informed neural networks, and multimodal fusion to extract insights from high-dimensional remote sensing data for precision agriculture and environmental monitoring. The goal? Detecting problems before they're visible to the naked eye. Currently exploring vision transformers, masked autoencoders, graph neural networks, and spatiotemporal modeling. Also fascinated by foundation models and Earth embeddings because understanding the entire planet through AI seemed like a reasonable challenge.
